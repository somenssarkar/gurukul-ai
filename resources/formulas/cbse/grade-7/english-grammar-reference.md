## 1. PARTS OF SPEECH

### Noun
**Definition:** Name of person, place, thing, or idea
- **Proper Noun:** Specific names (Rahul, India, Taj Mahal) — Always capitalize
- **Common Noun:** General names (boy, city, monument)
- **Collective Noun:** Group names (team, class, flock)
- **Material Noun:** Substances (gold, water, cotton)
- **Abstract Noun:** Ideas/qualities (honesty, love, courage)

### Pronoun
**Definition:** Replaces a noun
- **Personal:** I, you, he, she, it, we, they (subject forms); me, you, him, her, it, us, them (object forms)
- **Possessive:** my/mine, your/yours, his, her/hers, its, our/ours, their/theirs
- **Demonstrative:** this, that, these, those
- **Interrogative:** who, whom, whose, which, what
- **Relative:** who, whom, whose, which, that
- **Reflexive:** myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves

### Adjective
**Definition:** Describes a noun
- **Quality:** beautiful, kind, tall
- **Quantity:** some, much, little
- **Number:** one, two, first, second
- **Demonstrative:** this, that, these, those
- **Interrogative:** which, what, whose
- **Possessive:** my, your, his, her, its, our, their

**Degrees of Comparison:**
- Positive: tall, beautiful
- Comparative: taller, more beautiful (use -er for short words, more for long words)
- Superlative: tallest, most beautiful (use -est for short words, most for long words)

**Irregular Comparisons:**
- good → better → best
- bad → worse → worst
- little → less → least
- much/many → more → most

### Verb
**Definition:** Shows action or state
- **Transitive:** Takes an object (She ate an apple)
- **Intransitive:** No object (She sleeps)
- **Auxiliary:** Helping verbs (is, am, are, was, were, have, has, had, do, does, did, will, shall, can, may)

### Adverb
**Definition:** Modifies verb, adjective, or another adverb
- **Manner:** quickly, beautifully, well (How?)
- **Time:** now, today, yesterday, soon (When?)
- **Place:** here, there, everywhere (Where?)
- **Frequency:** always, often, never, seldom (How often?)
- **Degree:** very, quite, too, enough (To what extent?)

### Preposition
**Definition:** Shows relationship between noun/pronoun and another word
- **Time:** at, on, in, before, after, during, since, for
- **Place:** at, on, in, above, below, beside, between, among
- **Direction:** to, towards, into, from, through

### Conjunction
**Definition:** Joins words or clauses
- **Coordinating:** and, but, or, so (join equal parts)
- **Subordinating:** because, although, if, when, while (join dependent clause to independent)

### Interjection
**Definition:** Expresses sudden emotion
- Oh! Alas! Hurray! Wow! Ouch!

## 2. ARTICLES

### Indefinite Articles: a, an
- **a** before consonant **sounds** (a book, a university, a one-rupee coin)
- **an** before vowel **sounds** (an apple, an hour, an honest man)

### Definite Article: the
- **Use 'the':**
  - For specific/previously mentioned things (the book I bought)
  - With superlatives (the tallest boy)
  - With unique things (the sun, the moon, the Earth)
  - With rivers, oceans, mountain ranges (the Ganga, the Himalayas)

- **Omit 'the':**
  - General plural/uncountable nouns (Cats are animals. Water is essential.)
  - Going for purpose (go to school = to study, go to church = to pray)
  - With meals, games, languages (have breakfast, play cricket, speak Hindi)

## 3. TENSES

### PRESENT TENSES

**Simple Present**
- **Form:** base verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)
- **Use:** habits, universal truths, scheduled events
- **Example:** I play cricket. The sun rises in the east.

**Present Continuous**
- **Form:** am/is/are + verb-ing
- **Use:** actions happening now
- **Example:** I am studying right now.
- **Note:** Stative verbs (know, understand, love, have=possess) don't use continuous form

**Present Perfect**
- **Form:** has/have + past participle (V3)
- **Use:** completed action with present relevance, unspecified past time
- **Keywords:** already, yet, just, ever, never, since, for
- **Example:** I have finished my homework. She has lived here for five years.

**Present Perfect Continuous**
- **Form:** has/have + been + verb-ing
- **Use:** action started in past, still continuing
- **Keywords:** since, for
- **Example:** It has been raining since morning.

### PAST TENSES

**Simple Past**
- **Form:** verb + -ed (regular) or irregular form (went, ate, wrote)
- **Use:** completed action in past
- **Example:** I visited the Taj Mahal yesterday.

**Past Continuous**
- **Form:** was/were + verb-ing
- **Use:** action in progress at specific past time
- **Example:** At 8 PM, I was studying.

**Past Perfect**
- **Form:** had + past participle (V3)
- **Use:** action completed before another past action
- **Example:** The train had left when I reached the station.

**Past Perfect Continuous**
- **Form:** had + been + verb-ing
- **Use:** action continuing up to a point in past
- **Example:** I had been waiting for an hour when he arrived.

### FUTURE TENSES

**Simple Future**
- **Form:** will/shall + base verb
- **Use:** future actions, predictions
- **Example:** I will go to Delhi tomorrow.

**Future Continuous**
- **Form:** will/shall + be + verb-ing
- **Use:** action in progress at future time
- **Example:** This time tomorrow, I will be travelling.

**Future Perfect**
- **Form:** will/shall + have + past participle
- **Use:** action completed before future time
- **Example:** By next month, I will have finished this book.

## 4. SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT

**Rules:**
1. Singular subject → singular verb (He plays)
2. Plural subject → plural verb (They play)
3. **Each, every, either, neither** → singular verb (Each boy has a book)
4. **Collective nouns** → singular (team as unit) or plural (members): The team is winning / The team are arguing
5. **Neither...nor / Either...or** → verb agrees with nearest subject (Neither he nor they are coming)

## 5. ACTIVE & PASSIVE VOICE

### Conversion Rules

**Active:** Subject + Verb + Object
**Passive:** Object + be + V3 + by + Subject

**Examples:**
- Active: Priya wrote a letter.
- Passive: A letter was written by Priya.

**Tense Changes:**
- Simple Present: write → is/are written
- Present Continuous: is writing → is being written
- Simple Past: wrote → was/were written
- Present Perfect: has written → has been written
- Simple Future: will write → will be written
- Modals: can write → can be written

**When to Use Passive:**
- Doer unknown: The window was broken.
- Doer unimportant: Rice is grown in India.
- Focus on action/receiver: The Taj Mahal was built in 1653.

## 6. DIRECT & INDIRECT SPEECH

### Conversion Rules

**Direct:** Exact words in quotation marks
**Indirect:** Reported words without quotation marks

**Changes Required:**
1. **Remove quotation marks**
2. **Add 'that'** (for statements)
3. **Change pronouns:** I→he/she, you→I/we/they, my→his/her
4. **Backshift tenses** (if reporting verb is past):
   - Present → Past
   - Past → Past Perfect
   - Present Perfect → Past Perfect
   - will → would
   - can → could
   - may → might
5. **Change time/place words:**
   - today → that day
   - yesterday → the day before / the previous day
   - tomorrow → the next day / the following day
   - here → there
   - this → that
   - now → then

**Examples:**

**Statement:**
- Direct: He said, "I am busy."
- Indirect: He said that he was busy.

**Question:**
- Direct: She asked, "Are you coming?"
- Indirect: She asked if/whether I was coming.

**Command:**
- Direct: He said to me, "Close the door."
- Indirect: He told me to close the door.

## 7. SENTENCE TYPES

### By Purpose
1. **Declarative:** Makes a statement (The sky is blue.)
2. **Interrogative:** Asks a question (Is the sky blue?)
3. **Imperative:** Gives command/request (Close the door.)
4. **Exclamatory:** Shows strong emotion (What a beautiful day!)

### By Structure
1. **Simple:** One independent clause (I like mangoes.)
2. **Compound:** Two+ independent clauses joined by coordinating conjunction (I like mangoes, but my brother likes apples.)
3. **Complex:** Independent clause + dependent clause (I will come when you call me.)

---

## 8. PHRASES & CLAUSES

### Phrase
- Group of words without subject-verb combination
- Types: Noun phrase, verb phrase, adjective phrase, adverb phrase, prepositional phrase
- Example: "The girl with long hair" (prepositional phrase modifying 'girl')

### Clause
- Group of words with subject and verb
- **Independent Clause:** Can stand alone as a sentence (I went to school)
- **Dependent Clause:** Cannot stand alone (when I woke up)
  - **Noun Clause:** Acts as noun (What he said is true)
  - **Adjective Clause:** Modifies noun (The book that I read was interesting)
  - **Adverb Clause:** Modifies verb (I will come when you call)

---

## 9. PUNCTUATION MARKS

- **Period (.)**: End of declarative/imperative sentence
- **Question Mark (?)**: End of interrogative sentence
- **Exclamation Mark (!)**: End of exclamatory sentence or interjection
- **Comma (,)**: Separates items in list, after introductory phrase, before coordinating conjunction in compound sentence
- **Quotation Marks (" ")**: Direct speech
- **Apostrophe (')**: Possession (boy's book, boys' books) or contraction (it's = it is, don't = do not)
- **Colon (:)**: Introduces list or explanation
- **Semicolon (;)**: Joins closely related independent clauses

### Capitalization Rules
- First word of sentence
- Proper nouns (names of people, places, days, months)
- Pronoun 'I'
- Titles (Mr., Dr., Professor)
- First word of quoted speech

## 10. MODALS

| Modal | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **can** | ability, informal permission | I can swim. Can I go? |
| **could** | past ability, polite request | I could swim when I was 5. Could you help? |
| **may** | formal permission, possibility | May I enter? It may rain. |
| **might** | less likely possibility | It might rain tomorrow. |
| **must** | strong obligation, certainty | You must study. He must be tired. |
| **should** | advice, weak obligation | You should exercise daily. |
| **will** | future, willingness | I will help you. |
| **would** | polite request, past habit | Would you like tea? I would play here as a child. |

## 11. PREPOSITION COLLOCATIONS

**Common Combinations:**
- afraid **of**
- good **at** (skill/subject)
- fond **of**
- proud **of**
- married **to**
- different **from**
- similar **to**
- angry **with** (person) / angry **at** (thing)
- interested **in**
- capable **of**
- dependent **on**
- famous **for**
- superior **to**
- inferior **to**

**Common Errors to Avoid:**
- ~~reach to~~ → reach (no preposition needed)
- ~~discuss about~~ → discuss (already transitive)
- ~~married with~~ → married to
- ~~comprise of~~ → comprise (no 'of' needed)

## 12. COMMONLY CONFUSED WORDS

| Word 1 | Word 2 | Difference |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **accept** | **except** | accept = receive; except = exclude |
| **affect** | **effect** | affect = influence (verb); effect = result (noun) |
| **its** | **it's** | its = possessive; it's = it is |
| **their** | **there** / **they're** | their = possessive; there = place; they're = they are |
| **your** | **you're** | your = possessive; you're = you are |
| **lose** | **loose** | lose = misplace; loose = not tight |
| **than** | **then** | than = comparison; then = time |
| **principal** | **principle** | principal = head/main; principle = rule |
| **stationary** | **stationery** | stationary = not moving; stationery = writing materials |
| **complement** | **compliment** | complement = complete; compliment = praise |

## 14. SENTENCE TRANSFORMATION PATTERNS

**Affirmative ↔ Negative:**
- Affirmative: He is honest.
- Negative: He is not dishonest. (double negative = affirmative meaning)

**Simple ↔ Compound:**
- Simple: Being tired, he went to bed.
- Compound: He was tired, so he went to bed.

**Simple ↔ Complex:**
- Simple: On seeing me, he ran away.
- Complex: When he saw me, he ran away.

**Exclamatory ↔ Assertive:**
- Exclamatory: What a beautiful flower it is!
- Assertive: It is a very beautiful flower.

**Interrogative ↔ Assertive:**
- Interrogative: Who does not love his country?
- Assertive: Everyone loves his country. (rhetorical question → statement)

## 15. WRITING TIPS

**Sentence Construction:**
1. Every sentence needs subject + verb
2. Avoid sentence fragments (incomplete sentences)
3. Avoid run-on sentences (join with conjunction or split into two)
4. Vary sentence length for interest
5. Use active voice for clarity (unless passive is more appropriate)

**Paragraph Structure:**
1. Topic sentence (main idea)
2. Supporting sentences (details, examples)
3. Concluding sentence (wrap up)

**Letter Writing:**
- Formal: Dear Sir/Madam, Yours faithfully/sincerely
- Informal: Dear [Name], Yours lovingly/affectionately, Love
